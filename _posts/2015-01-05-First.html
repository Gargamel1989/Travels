My journey started on the 3rd of January in Brussels. Upon opening the front door, I was greeted by a moderate drizzle, but the weather forecast had assured me this would clear up by noon. After doing a little tourist tour around Brussels/discovering the joys of searching for the GR trail markings, making my first stop at a local coffee shop and saying my final goodbyes to two friends who had joined me up to this point, I was all by myself.

The weather did not improve by noon. It made crossing the ZoniÃ«nwoud an... interesting experience. The rain subdued for a little while after my emerging from the forest, only to return with renewed force not half an hour later. After 6 hours of cycling and walking in the rain, I was wet to the core and my morale was already at a low point. The sun was setting and I had yet to encounter any potential accommodation (obviously tent was not a feasible option at this point). Things got real a lot quicker than I expected...

{% include img.html url='img/start/drying' %} A nice place to watch the sunset and feel the panic flooding into your brain!

At that point I probably made the best decision of the day: Fuck the GR routes, head over to the nearest town and find myself a safe haven to regroup. The nearest hostel was in Namen (my planned goal of the day, but at this point still a good 40km away), so I rode my bike to the nearest train station and took the next train there.

After recovering my soaked underpants from halfway up my ass, the best low-power-shower in recent memory and a cold beer, I was ready to reconsider my route and planning. All in all not the best way to start a six month journey, and maybe I could have prevented some of these things, but then again, experience is the best teacher.

Lessons Learned:

  • I cannot bike 70km in one day with this pack on my back
  • If rain is a possibility, have accomodation plans in advance and have a backup plan
  • GR routes are not made for bycicles
  • My gear is waterproof, but not after an entire day of rain
