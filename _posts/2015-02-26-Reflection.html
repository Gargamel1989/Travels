Also I just couldn't get it to feel right, so I've basically deleted most of it multiple times and now what I'm left with is this short summary of Dijon-Lyon and a little reflection of the last month.

Also there will not be many pictures of mine in this post, because my fairphone fell and the camera is pretty much unusable now... Bummer. I managed to get it working for a little while in Lyon but then dropped it again and completely ruined it. I will figure something out for the future though.

Dijon to Lyon

Upon arriving in Dijon, I quickly realized this was not going to be Paris #2. A bland city with not much to see. Even mustard shops were nowhere to be found, which for me as a mustardlover was quite the disappointement... The famous old houses spread throughout the city were nice but unfortunately vastly overhyped by the tourist office.

Good things Dijon had to offer included:

  • An amazing shower
  • A halfway decent internet connection (halleluja)
  • 3 cool Belgian guys from Leuven with whom I played Chinese f*cking (it's a card game...) for the better part of the night.

I also discovered a French website for carsharing which turned out to be way cheaper than taking the train. I took a first carshare to Lyon after 3 days in Dijon. This was initially not on my planning, but thanks to a tip from my mom I decided to check it out anyway since it was still kind of on the way to Val d'Isère.

Lyon turned out to be absolutely amazing. Something beautiful behind every corner, an amazing hillside full of medieval (I think) buildings, a load of amazing architecture from every century imaginable and a massive Basilic overlooking the whole city.

My hostel was located at the top of the hill and provided a very nice chillout room with an awesome view. I spent a few days getting lost in the city streets and a few days inside resting my legs. All in all a great time. I decided to stay there until my departure to Val d'Isère.

Time for some premature reflections

  1. Camping

    2. Living in Regina... What can I say, we've had good days and lesser days, no horrible days yet. The one problem about living in a tent has been that you have no proper way to relax after a long day of walking. You can't pitch the tent until it goes dark (if you're wild camping in populated areas at least) and chilling in a bar for 3 hours every evening gets quite expensive.
    I'm hoping this will get better as the weather improves, because the cold basically prevents me from resting outside on benches etc. for too long.

  2. Biking and Walking

    3. By now everyone should know about my mostly self-inflicted bike troubles. I still have fond memories of the few massive downhills in the Champagne region, but in the end I am glad we parted ways. If not I would never have been able to make the beautiful trek through the Morvan Forest nor would I have made it to Lyon.
    From now on I will be relying on carsharing, public transit and hitchhiking for long distances and hiking for the natural areas.

  3. Finances

    4. I gave myself a budget constraint of €15 a day, knowing full well this would be very hard to keep up, but then again I perform better under stress... Most of the hostels in France cost about €20 a night, which meant I had to get really inventive with my food. Luckily I don't drink, so that gave me some wiggle room (no really, I've only had 3 beers and half a bottle of wine in the last month. I plan to make up for it now though :D).
    I have heard from multiple sources the Balkan (excluding Croatia) is quite a lot cheaper, so hopefully it won't get too much more out of control after the skiing bit.

  4. Logistics

    5. Living out of a trekking bag. It is horrible but humbling experience. Good planning and thourough knowledge of the packing order are definite requirements. So far I haven't lost a single item however, which I daresay is nothing short of a miracle.
    It's hard to say for sure so soon, but this might be having a good influence on my natural tendence for disorder.
