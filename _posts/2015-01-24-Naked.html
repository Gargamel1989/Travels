To keep the cost of my stay in Paris within reasonable limits, I had searched for a place to stay through couchsurfing. The first person that replied to my couchsurfing request came with a disclaimer saying he lived the nudist lifestyle and his guests should be open to trying it themselves. This was definitely out of my comfort zone, but sometimes that can be a good thing which leads to fun experiences and new insights. Upon my arrival, I was pretty much the worst possible guest imaginable. I ate his food and his medicine, took a shower and crashed. It was necessary.

The next morning both my knees were hurting, the fever had turned into a full blown flu and my shoulders were still blocked. Dr. Google told me the kneepain was a generic case of "Runner's knee", which would be resolved by taking inflammatory medicine, resting and stop being a little bitch. Luckily over the course of my stay the pain slowly subsided. The flu lasted for about 4 days, kept in check by a daily dose of Paracetamol. And finally the shoulders were just a bad case of sore muscles that faded by the end of the week. So my stay in Paris was pretty much a drug fueled tourist trip, the best kind of tourist trip.

The Louvre was the first place that really blew me away. I generally have a hard time enjoying art, mostly due to my limited knowledge of it. However, my expectations were exceeded the moment I stepped into the courtyard of this epic museum. A big shoutout to the audioguides because they were just perfect. Hard to explain but truly an enjoyable experience that I would recommend to everybody. I also may or may not have felt emotions about art once or twice during my visit. Am I a hipster now?

Especially those last two were way more impressive than I remembered from my last visit. Sorry I doubted you, Paris...

The rest of my stay was a combination of cooking for my host (unfortunately his anus did not handle my chili too well), resting my body, watching classic french movies, a three guy butt-naked party (no body fluids were exchanged), vin rouge, pain et fromage and of course trying to get my bike fixed (did not work). Pascal (my hosts name) offered to let me stay for 2 days longer than planned so I could really shake all the fever and fatigue, big thanks for that! Unfortunately I forgot to take any pictures of him, but I guess they would have needed some heavy blurring anyway.

Leaving Paris after 6 days felt a bit weird. It's funny how fast you become attached to these places...

Oh fuck yea, I forgot. The day before I arrived was the day of the Charlie Hebdo attacks. I saw the events unfold from the city where it was happening and saw it almost through French eyes.
